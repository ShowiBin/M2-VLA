M²-VLA: Boosting Vision-Language Models for Generalizable Manipulation via Layer Mixture and Meta-Skills

Siyao Xiao1,2, Yuhong Zhang1, Zhifang Liu1, Zihan Gao1, Jingye Zhang1, Sinwai Choo1, Dake Zhong1, Mengzhe Wang3, Xiao Lin4, Xianfeng Zhou1, Jia Jia2*, Haoqian Wang1*
1Tsinghua University
2Peng Cheng Laboratory
3Fudan University
4Synapath
*Corresponding Author
Paper Code arXiv

M²-VLA enables generalized Vision-Language Models to serve as powerful backbones for robotic manipulation. Our approach preserves the VLM's semantic understanding capabilities while achieving strong generalization on instruction-following and novel-object manipulation tasks through the Mixture of Layers (MoL) mechanism and Meta Skill Module (MSM).

Abstract

Current Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models predominantly rely on end-to-end fine-tuning. While effective, this paradigm compromises the inherent generalization capabilities of Vision-Language Models (VLMs) and incurs catastrophic forgetting. To address these limitations, we propose M²-VLA, which demonstrates that a generalized VLM is able to serve as a powerful backbone for robotic manipulation directly. However, it remains a key challenge to bridge the gap between the high-level semantic understanding of VLMs and the precise requirements of robotic control. To overcome this, we introduce the Mixture of Layers (MoL) strategy that selectively extracts task-critical information from dense semantic features. Furthermore, to facilitate efficient trajectory learning under constrained model capacity, we propose a Meta Skill Module (MSM) that integrates strong inductive biases. Extensive experiments in both simulated and real-world environments demonstrate the effectiveness of our approach. Furthermore, generalization and ablation studies validate the architecture's zero-shot capabilities and confirm the contribution of each key component. Our code and pre-trained models will be made publicly available.

Motivation

Current Vision-Language-Action (VLA) frameworks typically utilize pre-trained Vision-Language Model (VLM) backbones, fine-tuning their internal parameters on large-scale robotic datasets to repurpose the model's generation capabilities from language tokens to action tokens. However, this paradigm brings catastrophic forgetting, degrading the VLM's inherent semantic understanding and consequently limiting the VLA's generalizability.

As illustrated above, while state-of-the-art VLA models perform well on in-domain tasks, they struggle with novel instructions or objects and frequently lose their Visual Question Answering (VQA) capabilities. We argue that preserving semantic understanding is a fundamental prerequisite for robust manipulation. Therefore, we propose that a generalized VLM can be effectively adapted as the backbone for robotic manipulation without compromising its core capabilities.

Framework and Method

M²-VLA Framework: The model extracts visual and language features, concatenates them with learnable queries, and utilizes a pre-trained VLM as the perceptual backbone. Action generation is decoupled and performed via a denoising transformer head, with Mixture of Layers (MoL) performing feature extraction from VLM, and Meta Skill Module (MSM) improving model capacity.

Mixture of Layers (MoL): Adaptively integrates hierarchical features extracted at various layers of the VLM via three decoupled attention branches, dynamically filtering and aggregating task-relevant knowledge for downstream action generation.

Meta Skill Module (MSM): Augments the action head with a repository of perceptual-skill pairs, allowing retrieval and integration of relevant meta-skills for action refinement during training and inference.
